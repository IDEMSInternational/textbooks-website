<Section
  tone="tinted"
  title="Three Technology Layers, One Unified Architecture, Unlimited Custom Variants"
>

Open, Adaptive Textbooks are made possible by a 3-layer system that enables localisation and adaptation in a single deeply interoperable system.

The foundational Structured Library layer transforms open digital textbooks and resources into malleable, reusable structured data. The Customisation & Publishing layer allows educators to easily access, make sense of, and apply this complex structured data to create, publish and own locally adapted and customised textbooks variants from a single user-friendly platform.

The IDEMS Middleware layer makes it all possible: It creates, manages and maintains coherent meaning between the Structured Library's data foundations and unlimited published textbook variants to maintain systemic interoperability.

<Rows>
<Row eyebrow="Layer 1" title="Structured Library">

A continuously evolving library of expert-developed textbooks and teaching, learning, and assessment materials. Resources can be malleably re-structured and/or added to, down to the paragraph level, to create entirely new textbooks, and published simultaneously as website, app, chatbot, and/or printed textbook from a single source. Embedded open source products deliver additional resources and new layers of functionality, with new open software and products added to improve applied value.

</Row>
<Row eyebrow="Layer 2" title="IDEMS Middleware">

The core innovation, a middleware developed by IDEMS' mathematicians and mathematical scientists. This groundbreaking layer lets local educators easily develop custom digital textbooks and resources for their courses, contexts and students–and retain a living connection with a single unified **Open, Adaptive Digital Textbooks** architecture. A customisation anywhere becomes an innovation that can be used everywhere in a system with no duplication and no siloed forks.

</Row>
<Row eyebrow="Layer 3" title="Customisation & Publishing">

The textbooks platform hub. Educators at the national, regional, school, or classroom level, with the support of expert AI agents, compile context-specific textbooks (based on permission level if set by an existing education system)–and gain the ability to integrate their own resources and innovations into existing resources. Authors can see how their work is used by others to improve existing, or develop entirely new, textbooks and resources.

</Row>
</Rows>

</Section>

<Section
  centeredHead
  title="A Pioneering Middleware for Local Adaptation in a Global Textbooks Operating Environment"
>

The IDEMS Middleware layer brings together multiple innovations that work together to maintain quality standards, enable local adaptation and ownership, and facilitate global collaboration and continuous improvement.

<CardGrid columns={2}>
<Card title="The Textbooks Brain">

The "brains" of the textbooks operation (built on IDEMS' IP), this holds both open software/resources and complex meaning-making knowledge as structured, malleable data in a novel deterministic core. The expert-maintained core is the data foundation of responsible, auditable AI, which supports educators' textbook customisation and contributes to the Brain's continuous improvement.

</Card>
<Card title="Agentic AI Hands">

This suite of expert small language model AI agents are developed for narrow use cases, supporting textbook publishing, adaptation and iteration by non-technologist educators and authors. Trained on the Textbooks Brain's high-quality data, AI Hands are bounded, auditable, and resource efficient.

</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Card title="A Global Impact Operating Environment">

Together these power a novel **Open, Adaptive Digital Textbooks** Operating Environment.

Unlimited, low-cost local textbook adaptations and innovations, owned and controlled at the local level, maintain a living connection with a system of expert-maintained, accredited high-quality educational resources. It's a bounded digital textbooks ecosystem that enables multidirectional global-local collaboration and innovation.

The result is continuous improvement of teaching and learning resources at the systemic and local levels, for measurably improved student outcomes around the world.

</Card>

</Section>
