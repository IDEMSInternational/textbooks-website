<Section
  tone="tinted"
  eyebrow="Under the hood"
  title={"Three Technology Layers,\nOne Unified Architecture"}
  titleLineBreak
>

**Open, Adaptive Digital Textbooks** are made possible by a 3-layer system that enables localisation and adaptation in a single connected textbooks operating environment.

The foundational **Resources Library** layer holds the ever-expanding suite of high-quality and curated open textbooks, resources, and software that can be selectively shaped into local textbook versions.

The **Customisation & Publishing** layer allows educators to easily access, make sense of, and apply this complex structured data to create, publish and own locally adapted and customised textbooks variants from a single user-friendly platform.

The **IDEMS Middleware** layer makes it all possible: It transforms open digital textbooks and resources into malleable, reusable structured data. It creates, manages and maintains coherent meaning between structured Resource Library data and unlimited published textbook variants to maintain systemic interoperability.

</Section>

<Section
  tone="dark"
  eyebrow="Inclusive innovation"
  title="A New Model of Community-Centred Technology"
>

Software, resources, knowledge, relationships, sense making: all are data, held across communities and roles, at every level of expertise.

Even in large, centralised organisations, designing technology systems to manage this kind of data complexity is a hard problem. Across variable global educational contexts, it’s currently impossible. Existing technologies aren’t built for the distributed ownership and collaboration needed to unlock scattered data and make it accessible–and useful.

IDEMS’ innovation is a new model: technology designed to surface and apply the layered expertise of communities at scale, enabling the right people to affordably access what they need, take ownership to serve their context, and contribute back to a single system that benefits everyone.

</Section>
