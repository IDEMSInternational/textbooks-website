<Section
  eyebrow="The product"
  title="We're building a connected system for open, adaptive textbooks"
>

Open textbooks today are scattered, hard to adapt, and quickly out of date. We're
connecting them into a single system where any textbook can be adopted, adapted,
versioned and recombined — while staying maintained and up to date.

This is a great leap forward for open educational resources: not just a library,
but a living, interoperable system that grows with the community that uses it.

</Section>

share your own work, there's a way in.

<Section
  eyebrow="Why now"
  title="Deep interoperability is the secret sauce"
>

Some of what we do is structuring and operationalising — putting existing pieces
together in a way that's genuinely useful. But the real value comes from our
in-development **deep interoperability**: a new model of versioning and
adaptation that lets everything live in one connected system.

Built on the semantic language of PreTeXt as a single source of truth, it cuts
across different tools and formats — so a change in one place can flow everywhere
it's needed.

</Section>

<Section
  tone="dark"
  eyebrow="How it works"
  title="From one textbook to a whole connected system"
>

1. **Add and convert textbooks** — send us your Creative Commons–licensed
   textbook and we'll convert it to PreTeXt and add it to the library.
2. **Create foundational versions and variants** — turn a library textbook into
   a variant of a foundational version (say, a Jamovi variant of "Intro to R
   with Stats"), with the differences tracked by intent tagging.
3. **Let anyone create a new variant** based on an existing textbook.
4. **Combine components of different textbooks** into an entirely new variant —
   down to the individual section, chapter or assessment. You could give each
   student a textbook targeted to their needs and strengths.
5. **A whole, coherent system** that stays maintained, up to date, and always
   growing.

Our goal: a fully working version — steps 1 through 5 — within a year.

</Section>
